Evolution of communication

Okay, Electronics, Comupters, Internt, Telecommunication, Bloackchain, Artificial Intelligence, Star Wars. I love them all. Seriously, I do. We humans are visual beings. We think visually, we communicate visually, we do a whole bunch of things visually. In the non technical world it doesn’t really mean much. I mean, you wanted to talk your grandma, the good folks made a telephone. You wanted to call from anywhere, they gave you cellphones. But the innate desire to communicate visually, was still there. We share and talk in the language of experience where just listening to the voice is not enough, and hence, we got to the point where Internet (which was essentially invented as a giant index page for digital books back at CERN), got mainstream owing to scorny kids in dorm rooms who created “social netowrks”. There, we were talking visually now. Whoopdey-doo, grandma could now see that girl I was dating and could tell me her nose was awefully wide at the base. Why!?

But then we did what we’ve always done best. Blow up! Internet was just not enough. The photographs turned to videos and static pages turned to complex algorithm beasts with artificial intelligence, that could observe what I liked, when I liked and what my third cousin likes at 1 AM when he is geotagged in his dormroom. It could learn my behavioural patterns to the point that it started suggesting me where can I buy toilet paper and one-click delivery, right when I am at the can! That was creepy. All the data in the world right now is being ingested by these ginormous monopolies who have these hideous beasts (fine algorithms, whatever), and you’ve got no control over it, even if you wnated to. These automated systems, have hit the critical mass beyond which they are now self sustaining & healing to the effect that no amount of net neutrality campaigns are going change the fact that “they’re selling you”. Every year we generate more data than we can scalably consume and in past couple of years alone we have generated more data than entire humanity generated collectively so far. Organizations are thriving on it, and Internet of things is just a fancy way of saying “More data, more control”. Financial institutions are no different.

At it’s core, we were trying to find better, scalable ways of communicating. Better at communicating experience, words, data or .. assets. That’s what any of us has ever wanted. In 2007, Satoshi Nakamoto released a white paper highlighting a revolutionary new way of looking at internet in form of this decentralized interntet. A distributed ledger and a protocol for concensus to update it. A new age currency was born out of this proof of work consensus protocol, Bitcoin. Most of the people who talk about it, don’t know what it is and, if you really understand it’s core workings, it fundamentally challenges your ideas of fiat currency and frankly, what gives the currency it’s value? But underneath all those rebellious ideas, lies a fundamental breakthrough in communication, blockchain. That’s what it’s all about. All of a sudden, almost all of the intergalactic communication & asset transfer ideas seem doable (I mean you still gotta work out the quatum entanglement communication system or some fancy quantum communication sattelite network but yeah, whatever). Blockchain offers this new age internet a new dimension. Another dimention to balance disproportionate growth in older one, never hurt any scalability project. Sepcially if you are consider the tiny network of lets say billions .. of devices on the planet. Communicating with each other under no monopoly (trying to control the communication and filter in & out the good bits (good for their business)). A communication system, that can transfer assets & experiences in form of information & retain its ownership. It’s a bold new world out there, and boy, are we just getting started up! But this time, we are young, and smack in the middle of it’s birth. Most of you folks missed the dot com bubble, but are here, in your prime for this revolution evolution. The transistors, the cloud, the artificail intelligence and the god damn IoT; they’ve all been building upto to this. Blockchain, seems to be the missing peice in the puzzle.
